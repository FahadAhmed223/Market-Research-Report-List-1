<p><p>The heat-treated wheat flour market can be categorized into two main types: dry and wet. Dry heat-treated flour is processed using high temperatures to eliminate microbial activity and enhance shelf life, making it ideal for baked goods and snacks. In contrast, wet heat treatment involves using steam or moisture, which helps retain more of the flour's nutrients and alters its functional properties. Both types serve distinct purposes in food production, appealing to varying consumer preferences for texture and nutritional value.</p></p>
