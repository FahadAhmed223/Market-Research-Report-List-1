<p><ul><li>Interpositional Arthroplasty</li><li>Excisional Arthroplasty</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Arthroplasty encompasses surgical procedures aimed at restoring joint function through the reconstruction or replacement of damaged joints. Interpositional arthroplasty involves inserting a material between joint surfaces to facilitate movement and alleviate pain, often applied in cases like thumb or temporomandibular joint disorders. In contrast, excisional arthroplasty entails the removal of diseased joint tissue, preserving structural integrity while relieving symptoms, commonly used in arthritis or injury cases. Both types address joint dysfunction, catering to different patient needs and conditions.</p></p>
