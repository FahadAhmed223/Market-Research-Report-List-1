<p><p>The Pre-Clinical Chemistry Testing Device market for laboratory animal models encompasses two main types: In Vitro and In Vivo test devices. In Vitro test devices are utilized for conducting experiments outside living organisms, often using cell cultures or isolated tissues, allowing for controlled and rapid assessments of chemical effects. In contrast, In Vivo test devices involve testing within living organisms, providing insights into the pharmacokinetics, efficacy, and safety of compounds in a more complex biological environment. Both types are essential for drug development and safety evaluation.</p></p>
