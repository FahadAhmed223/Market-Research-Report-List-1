<p><p>The bread improvers market comprises various types, including emulsifiers, enzymes, oxidizing agents, and reducing agents, each enhancing bread quality. Emulsifiers improve dough stability and texture, while enzymes facilitate fermentation and regulate dough development. Oxidizing agents strengthen gluten structure, leading to improved volume and crumb uniformity, whereas reducing agents counteract gluten strength to enhance extensibility. Collectively, these components optimize dough performance, contribute to specific bread characteristics, and address varying production needs in the baking industry.</p></p>
