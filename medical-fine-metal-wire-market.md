<p><p>The medical fine metal wire market comprises various types, including stainless steel, nitinol, tungsten, silver, gold, and others. Stainless steel is widely used for its strength and corrosion resistance, particularly in surgical applications. Nitinol, an alloy of nickel and titanium, offers excellent elasticity and shape memory, making it ideal for stents and guidewires. Tungsten is favored for its high density and biocompatibility. Silver and gold are used in specialized applications, such as electrochemical sensors and implantable devices, due to their antimicrobial properties. Other materials may include alloys and biocompatible metals designed for specific medical uses.</p></p>
