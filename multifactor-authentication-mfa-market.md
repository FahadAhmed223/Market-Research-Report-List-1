<p><p>Multifactor Authentication (MFA) enhances security by requiring multiple verification methods before granting access. In Two-Factor Authentication (2FA), users provide two forms of identification, typically something they know (like a password) and something they have (like a mobile device). Three-Factor Authentication (3FA) adds another layer, often including a biometric factor, such as a fingerprint or facial recognition. Additionally, the market includes other MFA types that incorporate multiple authentication methods, combining tools like hardware tokens, smart cards, and additional contextual factors for improved security.</p></p>
