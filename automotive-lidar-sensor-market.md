<p><p>The Automotive LiDAR Sensor Market comprises two main types: Solid-State LiDAR and Mechanical/Scanning LiDAR. Solid-State LiDAR uses fixed components without moving parts, offering durability, compact design, and lower costs, making it ideal for mass-market adoption. Mechanical/Scanning LiDAR relies on rotating or moving parts to scan the environment, providing high-resolution, long-range detection but with higher complexity and cost. Both types are critical for autonomous vehicles, with Solid-State LiDAR focusing on affordability and reliability, while Mechanical LiDAR excels in precision and performance.</p></p>
