<p><p>The pipette tip market is primarily divided into filtered and non-filtered types. Filtered pipette tips are designed with a barrier that prevents contamination and aerosol transfer, making them ideal for sensitive applications like molecular biology and microbiology. Non-filtered pipette tips, on the other hand, are used for general laboratory tasks where contamination risks are lower. Both types cater to different user needs, with filtered tips providing added protection in critical experiments and non-filtered tips offering cost-effectiveness for routine tasks.</p></p>
