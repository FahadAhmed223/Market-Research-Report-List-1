<p><p>The insoluble dietary fibers market consists of various types, including cellulose, hemicellulose, lignin, and others. Cellulose, derived from plant cell walls, provides structure and aids digestion. Hemicellulose, a mix of sugars, complements cellulose by enhancing gut health. Lignin, a complex polymer, contributes to the rigidity of plant cells and supports digestive regularity. Other types may include chitin and resistant starch, which also offer health benefits. Together, these fibers play a critical role in nutrition and digestive wellness.</p></p>
