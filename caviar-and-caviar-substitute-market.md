<p><p>Caviar and caviar substitutes encompass a range of products differentiated by processing methods. Salted processing involves curing fish eggs with salt, enhancing flavor and extending shelf life. Pressed processing refers to compressing caviar to create a firmer texture, often used in gourmet dishes. Pasteurized processing heat-treats the eggs to ensure safety and prolonged storage, appealing to broader markets requiring longer shelf lives. Each method caters to varying consumer preferences for taste, texture, and preservation in the luxury seafood market.</p></p>
