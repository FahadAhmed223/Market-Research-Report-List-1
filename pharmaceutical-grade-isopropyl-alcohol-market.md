<p><p>The pharmaceutical grade isopropyl alcohol market encompasses various production methods, including propylene hydration, acetone hydrogenation, and isopropyl acetate hydrogenation. Propylene hydration involves the direct hydration of propylene to produce isopropyl alcohol. In acetone hydrogenation, acetone is hydrogenated to yield isopropyl alcohol, typically via catalytic processes. Isopropyl acetate hydrogenation involves the hydrogenation of isopropyl acetate to obtain isopropyl alcohol. Each method contributes to the supply of high-purity isopropyl alcohol used in pharmaceuticals, cosmetics, and sanitization products.</p></p>
