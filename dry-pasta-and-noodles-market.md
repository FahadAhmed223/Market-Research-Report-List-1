Durum wheat semolina, known for its high protein content, is commonly used to produce pasta, resulting in a firmer texture and favorable cooking qualities. In contrast, soft wheat flour, with lower protein levels, is often utilized for noodles, yielding a softer texture and versatility in dishes. Both types cater to diverse culinary preferences and dietary needs.</p></p>
