<p><p>The Fresh Beef Packaging market consists of various methods to extend shelf life and preserve quality. Modified Atmosphere Packaging (MAP) replaces air with protective gases, enhancing freshness. Vacuum Skin Packaging (VSP) tightly wraps meat in a skin-like film, reducing spoilage. Vacuum Thermoformed Packaging (VTP) involves heating and forming plastic around the product in a vacuum, ensuring a tight seal. Other methods may include tray sealing or barrier films, each contributing to food safety, consumer appeal, and reduced waste in the fresh beef supply chain.</p></p>
