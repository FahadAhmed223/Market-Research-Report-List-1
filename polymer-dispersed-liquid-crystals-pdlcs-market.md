<p><p>Polymer-Dispersed Liquid Crystals (PDLCs) are composite materials combining liquid crystals with a polymer matrix, allowing the manipulation of light transmission. Key market types include scattering PDLCs, which offer opacity and are commonly used in privacy glass, and nano-PDLCs, featuring nanoparticles for enhanced electro-optical performance and durability. Polymer network liquid crystals are structured to maintain stability and response, making them suitable for displays and smart windows. Each type caters to specific applications in various industries, including automotive, architecture, and consumer electronics.</p></p>
