<p><p>The anticholinergic drug market is categorized into three main types: oral, parenteral, and topical. Oral anticholinergics are typically taken as tablets or capsules, providing convenient dosing for conditions like overactive bladder or respiratory disorders. Parenteral anticholinergics are administered via injection, offering rapid action for acute situations, such as managing muscle spasms. Topical anticholinergics are applied directly to the skin, useful for localized treatments such as motion sickness patches, allowing targeted relief with minimal systemic effects.</p></p>
