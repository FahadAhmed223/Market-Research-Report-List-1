<p><p>Embedded Subscriber Identity Module (e-SIM) technology allows devices to connect to cellular networks without needing a physical SIM card. The e-SIM market primarily consists of two types: e-SIM cards and e-SIM chips. e-SIM cards are integrated into devices, providing flexibility for users to switch carriers without physically replacing the SIM. e-SIM chips, on the other hand, are the embedded components that enable e-SIM functionality in various devices, including smartphones, wearables, and IoT devices, fostering seamless connectivity.</p></p>
