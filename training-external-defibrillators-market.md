<p><p>The training external defibrillator market is divided into two primary types: automatic and manual defibrillators. Automatic external defibrillators (AEDs) are user-friendly devices designed for laypersons, featuring voice prompts and automated analysis to guide users through the defibrillation process. In contrast, manual defibrillators are typically used by trained medical professionals, requiring them to interpret heart rhythms and deliver shocks based on their assessment. Both types play crucial roles in emergency response and cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) training.</p></p>
